📄 Transcript — teacher reference / post-listening

Neil & Sam: Did you make any New Year's resolutions — promises to do something positive this year? Neil makes the same one every year: to exercise more and get fit. But he's too lazy to go running on cold mornings — he'd rather stay in bed. This is a "lazy person's guide": what is the least exercise you can get away with and still stay healthy?

Zoe (BBC Radio 4, Inside Health): Push yourself — if you can swim a length in 40 seconds, try 35. You exercise harder but for a shorter time: that's a trade-off (you accept a disadvantage to get a benefit). Many jobs are sedentary — they involve little physical activity. So look for easy wins (tasks that are easy to do and bring benefits): active travel, parking further away, walking from the car, using the stairs instead of the lift. Even walking faster helps — instead of 10,000 steps, can you do 5,000 steps faster?

Short, sharp sessions give you more bang for your buck — better results for the same effort. But remember, the body needs rest too!

Answer: the UK government recommends 10,000 steps a day (though walking fewer steps faster also has benefits).
