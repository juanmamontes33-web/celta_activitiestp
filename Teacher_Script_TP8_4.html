Teacher Script · TP8 · Writing (Functional Language)

Travel Brochure Writing Lesson

Model: “Puerto Lucía — Your Perfect Escape” ✈️

⏱️ 45 min📈 Upper-Intermediate (B2)💻 Zoom · ~8 Ss✍️ Process writing

🎯 Keep these visible

  • Lead-in: annotate Ss’ ideas on the shared slide as they speak.
  • Guided discovery: Ss work in PAIRS in breakout rooms during the task (not alone).
  • Instructions short; ICQ the mechanics before every task; CCQs come after the discovery.
  • Writing: monitor live and give on-the-spot feedback on BOTH content and language.
  • Protect the 12-min writing floor; keep feedback scalable (peer checklist + short DEC); selective nomination.
1 · Lead-inset the context3 min
Do

Share Slide 2 (four destination photos + two questions).

Teacher says

“Look at these four places. Which one would YOU like to visit? Why?”

Do — annotate

Nominate 2–3 Ss (selective). As they answer, type or annotate their ideas directly on the slide (Zoom annotate / a text box) so their contributions are visible.

Teacher says

“Now — what kind of text is a brochure? Where do you see them? What are they for?”

Elicit: a brochure / leaflet; at hotels, travel agencies, online; its job is to persuade you to visit or buy.
Teacher says — bridge

“Exactly — today you’re going to write your OWN travel brochure. Let’s look at a good one first.”

Keep it to 3 minutes. The photos + annotation just warm them up and surface the genre. Don’t let the discussion run.
2 · Model Analysisgist + layout10 min
▸ Gist (3 min)
Do

Paste the Student Activity link. Show Slide 3 (the model + question).

Teacher says

“Read the brochure quickly. Just one question: what is it FOR? Choose A, B or C.”

ICQs (mechanics, then time): “Read fast or slowly?” → Fast. · “One answer or three?” → One. · “How long? Two minutes.”
Do

Ss read individually. OCFB — nominate 1 S.

Answer: B — to persuade you to visit. “How do you know?” [positive words, ‘book today’].
▸ Layout (7 min)
Teacher says

“A brochure has parts. Look — the text is in 5 sections, A to E. Match each section to its NAME. Do it with your partner.”

ICQ: “Do you match names to sections, or write a brochure now?” → Match. · “Alone or with a partner?” → Partner.
Do

Pairs in breakout rooms (3 min). Monitor. Then reveal Slide 4a.

Key: A = Title · B = Introduction (hook) · C = Things to see & do · D = Practical information · E = Call to action.
CCQs on each (elicit): “Which makes you WANT to go?” [B] · “Which tells you HOW to get there & prices?” [D] · “Which tells you to DO something now?” [E] · “Which NAMES the place?” [A] · “Which lists ACTIVITIES?” [C]
3 · Formulaic Languageguided discovery10 min
Ss DISCOVER in pairs — you set up, monitor silently, then elicit & concept-check. CCQs come AFTER the task.
Teacher says

“In the brochure there are four phrases that make it sound persuasive. In the activity: match each phrase to its JOB. Then, for each part of the sentence, label WHAT KIND of word or phrase it is. Work with your partner.”

ICQ: “Alone or with a partner?” → Partner. · “Do I give you the answers?” → No — we decide.
Do

Pairs in breakout rooms (4–5 min). Monitor silently. Then reveal Slide 5a and elicit.

OCFB + CCQs AFTER the task:
Nestled in the heart of… → “Does it tell us WHERE?” [Yes] · “Promotional writing or casual chat?” [Promotional]
Whether you’re… everyone → “Only for one type of person?” [No — everyone]
Don’t miss… → “Important to see?” [Yes] · “An order or a strong recommendation?” [Recommendation]
Why not…? → “A real question?” [No — a friendly suggestion]
Label the parts — elicit the category of each chunk:
• the coast = PLACE · Puerto Lucía = SUBJECT · offers golden beaches… = VERB + COMPLEMENT
• looking for adventure & just relaxing = both GERUND (-ING) PHRASES (same form)
• the Saturday night food festival = NOUN PHRASE — NOT “to + verb”
• treat yourself = BASE VERB · to a weekend away = OBJECT — NOT “to + verb”
Tip: the chunks are given — Ss decide the word type of each. Watch phrase 2: the decoys are infinitive & past participle — the classic Spanish-L1 gerund/infinitive slip (“just to relax”). Quick CCQ: “After ‘whether you’re’ — relax, to relax or relaxing?” [relaxing].
Appropriacy (not pronunciation): all four are persuasive, written, reader-facing. Flag register, not stress.
4 · Writingthe product12 min
Do

Show Slide 6 (checklist) + paste the shared Google Slides link. Model communicatively.

Teacher says — communicative demo

“I’ll write about my town. How could I start? Help me — ‘Nestled in the heart of…’ …of what? [elicit]. Great. Now YOU write a brochure about a place YOU know. Go to your own slide.”

ICQs (mechanics + checklist): “Do you write on your own slide?” → Yes. · “How many sections?” → Title + 3 + call to action. · “How long?” → 12 minutes.
Do — monitor + on-the-spot FB

Ss write on their slide (hard 12-min floor). Watch the filmstrip live. Drop comments giving feedback on content (Is it persuasive? Are the sections there?) AND language (accuracy + the target phrases). Note 2–3 points for DEC.

Don’t rescue too early. Give thinking space; comment where it moves the writing forward. Protect this stage — it’s the productive core.
5 · Feedbackpeer + DEC6 min
Do

Show Slide 7 (peer checklist).

Teacher says

“Now read your partner’s brochure. Use the checklist — tick what they did. Then tell them one thing you liked and one thing to improve.”

ICQs: “Writing or reading?” → Reading. · “Do you fix the text or give feedback?” → Give feedback. · “How long? 3 minutes.”
Do

Pairs in breakout rooms (3 min). Monitor.

▸ OCFB + DEC (2–3 min)
Teacher says

“[Student], what did your partner do WELL?” [nominate 1–2, selective]. “Now — a few things I saw. Are these correct? How do we fix them?”

Do

Show 2–3 anonymised points (content OR language) noted while monitoring; elicit corrections first, then confirm.

Teacher says — close

“Lovely brochures today — you could genuinely publish some of these. See you next class!” 🎉

Timing: 3 + 10 + 10 + 12 + 6 = 41 min · if you overrun, trim the layout OCFB or the demo — never the 12-min writing.
