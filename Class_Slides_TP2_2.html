Key Concept
What is a collocation?
And why DO? Why MAKE?
A collocation = two or more words that always go together in English.
Learn them as a pair — like one word! 🔗
✅ MAKE a mistake     ❌ DO a mistake
DO — tasks & activities
Things you perform — work, study, effort
do your homework do a project do well in an exam
MAKE — create & produce
Things you create or that produce a result
make a phone call make a mistake make friends
⚠️ Important for Spanish speakers!
"Hacer" in Spanish = both DO and MAKE in English. Don't translate — memorise the pair as one fixed unit!

Let's look closer — 3 key collocations

Meaning · Form · Pronunciation

12 min ⏱️
📞
make a phone call
Meaning: Use a phone to talk to someone.
Form:
make + a + phone call
Past: made a phone call
/ˌmeɪk ə ˈfəʊn kɔːl/
Stress on PHONE 📍
"a" = schwa /ə/ (weak!)
😅
make a mistake
Meaning: Do something wrong by accident.
Form:
make + a + mistake
Past: made a mistake
/ˌmeɪk ə mɪˈsteɪk/
Stress on -TAKE ⚠️
(not mis-)
📚
do your homework
Meaning: Complete the school work the teacher gives you to do at home.
Form:
do + your/my + homework
⚠ NO "a"! Uncountable!
/ˌduː jə ˈhəʊmwɜːk/
Stress on HOME 🏠
"your" = /jə/ (weak)
