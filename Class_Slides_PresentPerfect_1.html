How do we build it? — have / has + V3

Choose have or has, then add the past participle (the "3rd form").

He / She / It

⚡ Short forms: I've · you've · he's · she's · we've · they've

⚠️ Present perfect uses the 3rd form (V3) — NOT the past form! Say "I have eaten", not "I have ate".
❓

Questions & negatives — ever / never

ever = at any time in your life · never = at no time in your life.

Form ❓
❓ QUESTION (with ever)
Have you ever eaten sushi?
Have/Has + subject + ever + V3 ?
✔️ SHORT ANSWERS
Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.
Don't repeat the whole verb!
➖ NEGATIVE
I haven't been to China.
haven't / hasn't + V3
🚫 NEVER (already negative!)
I have never been to China.
have + never + V3 (no "not"!)
💡 "I haven't been" and "I have never been" mean the same — but never is stronger. Don't say "I haven't never been"!
🗂️

Past participles — the tricky bit!

Regular ones are easy. Irregular ones you just have to learn.

V3 🗂️
✅ Regular → just add -ed
travel → travelled
visit → visited
play → played
try → tried
Same as the past simple — easy! 🙂
⚠️ Irregular → memorise these!
be → been
eat → eaten
go → gone
see → seen
do → done
have → had
take → taken
write → written
swim → swum
drink → drunk
🧭 been vs gone: "He has been to Paris" = he went and came back (experience ✓). "He has gone to Paris" = he's there now / on his way. For experiences, use been to!
Pronunciation · listen & repeat
Squeeze it together! 🔊
Native speakers use short forms and make "have" weak. Don't say every word slowly!
SHORT FORMS
I've → /aɪv/
he's → /hiːz/
they've → /ðeɪv/
"Have you ever…?"
→ /həv juː ˈevə/
"have" is quiet, "EVER" is strong
