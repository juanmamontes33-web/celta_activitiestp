Look at the pattern

Every sentence has the same two parts.

Notice 🔎
If the weather is good, we'll go to the beach.
↑ CONDITION (present)
↑ RESULT (will)
❓ Is it certain, or does it depend on the condition?
❓ Is this about the past or a possible future?
💡

What does it mean?

A possible future + its result. Condition → consequence.

Meaning 🧭
🌧️
IF it rains
(maybe — it's possible)
➡️
🎬
we'll watch movies
(the result)
❓ Is it raining now?
❓ Is it possible it will rain?
❓ The movies — sure, or does it depend?
🇪🇸 Like Spanish: "Si llueve, veremos películas" — si + presente, futuro. Same idea!
🔧

How do we build it?

If + present simple , will + base verb

Form 🔧
If + present simple , will + base verb
"If you study, you'll pass."
🔀 You can swap the order:
"You'll pass if you study."
(No comma when the result comes first.)
⛔ Never use will in the IF-part!
"If it will rain…" ❌
"If it rains…" ✅

Speaking — The Conditional Chain!

Each result becomes the next condition. How long can you go?

Chain 🔗
How to play 🔗
🧑 Player 1: "If I'm hungry, I'll eat pizza."
👩 Player 2: "If you eat pizza, you'll feel happy."
🧑 Player 1: "If you feel happy, you'll dance."
👩 Player 2: "If you dance, …" → keep going! 🔁
⛓️ The result of one sentence becomes the condition of the next. If you make a mistake or stop — the chain breaks!
