Comparatives · Part 202 / 11
PATTERN · 1
as … as · saying two things are the same
= THE SAME
"Tom is as tall as Sam."
→ same height. Pattern: as + adjective + as
≠ NOT THE SAME
"This phone isn't as expensive as that one."
→ this one is cheaper. not as … as
💡 Tip: the adjective stays in its base form — "as tall as", NOT "as taller as"!
❓ "Tom is as tall as Sam" — same height or different? Same  ·  ❓ "not as expensive" — cheaper or dearer? Cheaper
Comparatives · Part 203 / 11
PATTERN · 2
less … than · the opposite of more
less = the reverse of more ↔️
"The village is less crowded than the city."
"This book is less interesting than the film."
Pattern: less + adjective + than   (usually with longer adjectives)
💡 Tip: with short adjectives, "not as … as" sounds more natural than "less": say "not as cheap as", not "less cheap".
Comparatives · Part 204 / 11
PATTERN · 3
Adverbs · comparing HOW we do things
Adverbs describe verbs — how you do an action:
SHORT → -ER
fast → faster
hard → harder
early → earlier
-LY → MORE
carefully → more carefully
slowly → more slowly
clearly → more clearly
IRREGULAR
well → better
badly → worse
"He drives more carefully than his brother." · "You did better than last time!"
💡 Tip: don't use the adjective for actions — "He drives more careful" → "more carefully". 🚗
Comparatives · Part 205 / 11
PATTERN · 4
the … , the … · things change together
"The more you practise, the better you get."
→ more practice = better result. They rise together.
"The older I get, the happier I am."
"The sooner we leave, the earlier we'll arrive."
💡 Bonus: for a change that keeps growing, use comparative + and + comparative: "It's getting hotter and hotter." · "Prices are getting more and more expensive." 🔥
Comparatives · Part 206 / 11
TIPS
Five tips to sound natural
1
"not as … as" is often more natural than "less" — "not as good as" > "less good".
2
Adverbs compare actions — most take more + -ly: "more carefully".
3
In "as … as", keep the adjective base: "as tall as" — never "as taller as".
4
"The more…, the better…" needs two "the"s — great for giving advice.
5
🔊 Pronunciation: keep as /əz/ and than /ðən/ weak and quick — stress the adjective or adverb, not these little words.

READ ALOUD
Read in pairs · the new patterns + pronunciation
💬 Choosing a gym
A: This gym is more modern, but it's not as cheap as the old one.
B: True, but the trainers explain things more clearly.
A: Yeah — and the more I go, the better I feel!
B: Exactly. The old gym wasn't as friendly as this one.
A: Let's join! The sooner we start, the fitter we'll be.
💬 Two coworkers
A: You finished the report faster than me!
B: I work better in the mornings. The earlier I start, the more I get done.
A: I'm the opposite — the later it gets, the harder I work!
B: Ha! Your way isn't as healthy as mine, though.
🎯 Swap roles and read again. Keep as /əz/ and than /ðən/ weak — stress the adjective / adverb.
