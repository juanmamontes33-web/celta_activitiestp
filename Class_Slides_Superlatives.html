Superlatives · Part 102 / 10
MEANING
Comparative vs superlative
2 THINGS → COMPARATIVE
"Coffee is healthier than cola."
→ comparing two drinks.
3+ / THE WHOLE GROUP → SUPERLATIVE
"Water is the healthiest drink."
→ number 1 of all drinks. 🏆
💡 A superlative picks the top (or bottom) of a group — and it always needs the.
❓ "the healthiest drink" — comparing two, or all of them? All  ·  ❓ Is anything healthier? No — it's #1
Superlatives · Part 103 / 10
FORM · 1
Short adjectives · the … -est
Short adjectives → the + adjective + -est:
cheap → the cheapest  ·  fast → the fastest  ·  old → the oldest
⚠ Same spelling rules as comparatives:
-e → -st
nice → the nicest
large → the largest
double consonant
big → the biggest
fit → the fittest
-y → -iest 🩺
healthy → the healthiest
easy → the easiest
Superlatives · Part 104 / 10
FORM · 2
Long adjectives, irregulars & "the least"
Long adjectives → the most + adjective:
important → the most important  ·  effective → the most effective
good → the best
bad → the worst
far → the furthest
💡 the least = the opposite of the most: "Fast food is the least healthy option on the menu."
Superlatives · Part 105 / 10
SOUND
Say it naturally
-EST = /ɪst/
healthiest /ˈhelθiɪst/
weak ending — don't over-stress it
the = /ðə/
the most /ðə məʊst/
soft "th" 👅 — quick and weak
STRESS
the HEALTHiest
on the adjective root
↻ DRILL — repeat
"Water is the HEALTHiest drink." · "Sleep is the most imPORtant habit."
Superlatives · Part 106 / 10
READ ALOUD
Read in pairs · superlatives + pronunciation
💬 Getting fit
A: What's the best exercise for beginners?
B: Walking! It's the easiest and the cheapest — no gym needed.
A: And swimming is one of the healthiest, isn't it?
B: Yes. But for me, sleep is the most important thing of all.
A: And the worst habit?
B: Sugar — the least healthy thing in my diet!
💬 At the café
A: This smoothie is the most delicious drink on the menu!
B: And it's the healthiest, too. 🥤
A: What's the worst choice here?
B: The chocolate cake — the tastiest, but the least healthy! 🍫
🎯 Swap roles and read again. Keep the /ðə/ weak, -est soft — stress the adjective.
