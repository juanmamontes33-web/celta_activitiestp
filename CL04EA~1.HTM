FORM
How it works
Imaginary → two clauses 💭
If I had a bigger house, I would invite everyone over.
If + past simple  ·  would + base verb
Meaning
Not real now — imaginary or unlikely situations.
Good to know
were for everyone · could/might = possible · never will/would after if!

Why the past tense?
The past form doesn't mean past time here — it signals "this isn't real". If I had a mansion… (but I don't).

LANGUAGE
Language feedback · MFP
Get it right 🛠️
Common errors
If I will have → If I had · I will buy → I would buy · would bought → would buy
No will after if
If-clause = past simple only.
Result = would / could / might + verb.
Pronunciation
Contract it: I 'd buy /aɪd/ · weak would → /wəd/.
Punctuation
Comma when if comes first. No comma when it's second: I'd move if I could.
