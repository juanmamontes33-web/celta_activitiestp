📄 Transcript — teacher reference / read aloud

Dan (host): Welcome to Pounds & Sense, the show about everyday money. Today Ana tells us how she took control of her finances. Ana, what went wrong at first?

Ana: Well, when I got my first job, I spent everything. Every payday I'd buy clothes, eat out, take taxis. By the end of the month I was broke, and slowly I slipped into debt — about two thousand pounds on my credit card.

Dan: That sounds stressful. What changed?

Ana: One day my card was declined in a shop. I was so embarrassed. That night I sat down and wrote a budget for the first time. I listed my income and every single expense.

Dan: And what did you find?

Ana: I was shocked — I was spending nearly a hundred pounds a month just on coffee and takeaways! So I started cooking at home and making coffee before work. Small changes, but they added up.

Dan: How long did it take to pay off the debt?

Ana: About a year. Every month I paid a little extra, and I put twenty percent of my salary into savings. It wasn't easy, but seeing the debt go down kept me motivated.

Dan: Any advice for our listeners?

Ana: Track everything — you can't manage money you don't understand. And be patient: you won't get rich overnight, but small habits really do change your life.

Dan: Brilliant. Ana, thank you.
