😱 A Strange Night
Last Saturday, Daniel was having a quiet evening at home. He was watching a film and eating popcorn when suddenly the lights went out. While he was looking for his phone, he heard a strange noise in the kitchen. His heart was beating fast. He slowly walked to the kitchen and opened the door. How do we build it? — Form

Past continuous = was/were + verb-ing. Watch the spelling!

Form 🔧
➕ Affirmative
I / He / She / It
was watching
You / We / They
were watching
➖ Negative / ❓ Question
➖ She wasn't sleeping.
❓ Were you working? — Was he eating?
✏️ Spelling: how to add -ing
1 · Most verbs → just add -ing
watch → watching · play → playing · do → doing
Why: the verb doesn't change — easy! 🙂
2 · Ends in -e → drop the e, add -ing
make → making · write → writing · have → having
Why: the e is silent, so we remove it — never makeing.
3 · Short verb (vowel + consonant) → double it
run → running · sit → sitting · swim → swimming
Why: doubling keeps the vowel short — runing would sound like "ruining".
⚠️ Don't double w / x / y: snow → snowing, fix → fixing, play → playing.

when vs while — the connectors

They join the two actions. Which verb comes after each one?

Link 🔗
WHEN
usually + the short action (Past Simple)
I was sleeping when the phone rang.
when + 🟧 finished action
WHILE
usually + the long action (Past Continuous)
While I was sleeping, the phone rang.
while + 🟪 action in progress
✨ Both sentences mean the same thing! The order can change — just remember the comma when you start with While…
Pronunciation · listen & repeat
The secret of was & were 🔊
In normal speech, was and were are weak and quiet — the -ing verb is strong.
WAS
was → /wəz/
"I wəz WATCHING TV"
WERE
were → /wə/
"They wə RUNNING"
DRILL — repeat after your teacher (chorus, then one by one):
🔁 "What wə you doing?"   /wɒt wə jʊ ˈduːɪŋ/
🔁 "I wəz cooking dinner."   /aɪ wəz ˈkʊkɪŋ/
🔁 "We wə sleeping."   /wiː wə ˈsliːpɪŋ/

Speaking game — "Alibi!" 🕵️

A crime happened last night at 8 p.m. Where were you? Prove it!

Speak 🎤
The story:
🚨 Last night someone ate all the cake in the office at 8 p.m. You are a suspect! Your teacher is the detective.

You must explain: where you were and what you were doing at 8 p.m. — with lots of detail!
Useful language:
"At 8 o'clock I was…"
"While I was ___, my friend was ___"
"I was ___ when ___ happened."
🕵️ Detective asks: "What were you doing? Who were you with? What was the weather like?" — catch them if they slip into past simple by mistake!
