🟢 have been + -ing for + period since + point in time
💡

When do we use it?

Started in the past… and STILL happening now.

Meaning 🧭
I have been living here for three years.
❓ Did it start in the past? Is it still happening now?
🇪🇸 Like "llevo tres años viviendo aquí" — same idea!
🔧

How do we build it?

have / has + been + verb-ing

Form 🔧
➕ AFFIRMATIVE
I / You / We / They → have been working
He / She / It → has been working
Short: I've been · she's been · they've been
❓ QUESTION & ➖ NEGATIVE
How long have you been learning?
I haven't been sleeping well.
🔊 Say it fast: "How long have you been…?" → /haʊ lɒŋ əv juː bɪn/ — "have been" is quiet and quick!
⏳

for vs since — say HOW LONG

The two words you need to answer "How long?"

Duration ⏳
FOR
+ a period of time (how long)
for 3 years for two hours for a long time
SINCE
+ a point in time (when it started)
since 2020 since Monday since I was a child
"I've been studying for two hours."  =  "I've been studying since 4 o'clock." ✅

Speaking 2 — Detective!

Look at your partner. Guess what they've been doing!

Game 🕵️
How to play 🕵️
Look at your partner's face / energy and guess:
🟣 "You look tired. Have you been working a lot?"
🟣 "You look happy! Have you been on holiday?"

Your partner says if you're right or wrong — and the truth!
Useful sentences
"You look… Have you been …-ing?"
"Yes, I have! I've been…"
"No, I haven't. Actually, I've been…"
