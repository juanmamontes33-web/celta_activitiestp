The Certainty Line — Answers

Check · Meaning

Answer key 🔑
couldn’t have
sure it did NOT happen
might / may / could have
possible — I’m not sure
must have
almost sure it happened
All three are guesses based on evidence — not facts we know for certain.

The Pattern — Answer

Check · Form

Answer key 🔑
modal + have + past participle (V3)
must / might / may / could / couldn’t  ·  left, gone, had, been…
have = an auxiliary (a helper verb — no meaning of “possess” here)  ·  after it → the past participle (3rd form: gone, left, had, been)
✅ have never changes (never has)
❌ NOT “must of” · NOT “must went”

…what did you hear?

Check & drill · Pronunciation

Answer key 🔑
He MUST’ve LEFT in a hurry.   /məstəv/
He MIGHT’ve GONE on holiday.   /maɪtəv/
He COULDN’T’ve GONE far.   /kʊdəntəv/
⬆️ Stress the modal + the main verb. The little words disappear.
🔉 have → /əv/ (“must’ve”). We write have, but we say /əv/ — never “must of”.
🗣️ Drill: left → must’ve left → He must’ve left.   Choral ×3 → individuals.
