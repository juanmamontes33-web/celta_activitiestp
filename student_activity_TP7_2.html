📖 The Story

Last Tuesday, Mrs Patterson finally knocked on the door of Flat 4B. Daniel, the quiet man who lived there, hadn’t been seen for three days. His curtains were shut, his post was piling up on the mat, and a cat was crying in the hallway.

When the building manager unlocked the door, the flat told a strange story. Daniel’s laptop was still switched on, with a half-finished email glowing on the screen. A cup of coffee sat cold on the desk. His coat and keys were missing, but his phone lay on the kitchen table, completely dead. The front door had been locked — from the outside.

The neighbours had plenty of theories.

“He must have left in a hurry,” said Mrs Patterson. “Nobody leaves a half-written email behind on purpose.”

The manager wasn’t so sure. “He might have gone on holiday,” he suggested. “Or he may have had some kind of family emergency.”

But old Mr Díaz from 4A shook his head. “He couldn’t have gone far without his phone,” he said quietly. “He could have had an accident — something’s wrong here.”

Three days later, Daniel posted a photo on Instagram — from a beach in Thailand. The cat, it turned out, wasn’t even his.
