Two futures — what's the difference?

Both talk about the future… but not in the same way.

Notice 🔎
🟡 "I'm going to visit my family."
❓ Did B decide this before the chat?
❓ Is it a plan?
🔵 "I know — I'll come with you!"
❓ Did A decide before, or right now?
❓ Is it a sudden decision?
📅

be going to — your plans

Decisions you already made + predictions you can SEE.

going to 📅
WE USE IT FOR
📅 plans / intentions (decided before):
"I'm going to study medicine."
👀 predictions from evidence:
"Look out — it's going to fall!"
FORM
am / is / are + going to + verb
I'm · she's · they're going to travel
🇪🇸 exactly like "voy a viajar" — ir a + infinitivo!
⚡

will — decide now & predict

On-the-spot decisions, opinions, promises, offers.

will ⚡
WE USE IT FOR
⚡ instant decisions: "I'm tired — I'll go to bed."
💭 opinions/predictions: "I think you'll love it."
🤝 promises/offers: "I'll help you!"
FORM
will + base verb (all people!)
I'll go · she'll go · won't = will not
⛔ never "will to" — I will to go → I will go
⚖️

So… which one?

Already decided, or deciding right now?

Choose 🧭
🟡 Use going to when…
✓ it's a plan (decided before)
✓ you can see evidence ("look!")
🔵 Use will when…
✓ you decide now (in the moment)
✓ it's an opinion ("I think…")
📅 "I'm going to cook tonight." (planned)  vs  ⚡ "I'm hungry — I'll cook something!" (decided now)
