M

A
Anna: That sounds lovely! Are you going to do anything special after lunch?
M
Marco: Yes — I'm going to visit the new art exhibition. I really want to see it. What about you? What are you going to do?
A
Anna: I'm not sure yet. I think I'm going to relax at home. But on Sunday I'm playing tennis with my sister at nine o'clock — we booked the court last week.
M
Marco: Nice! Are you going to watch the football match on Sunday evening too?
A
Anna: Maybe — I haven't decided. Oh, I almost forgot: I'm seeing the dentist on Monday at eight o'clock, so I'm not staying out late.
M
Marco: Good plan. Have a great weekend — I'm going to call you on Sunday to tell you about the exhibition!

Did you discover these? Plans & intentions

Clarify ✏️
😊 Meaning
A future plan / intention you have decided to do.
CCQs:
• Now or future? [Future]
• Have I decided? [Yes]
• Exact time arranged? [Not always]
🔧 Form
subject + am/is/are
+ going to + base verb
+ I'm going to visit
− She isn't going to study
? Are you going to watch…?
⚠ going to + BASE verb (NOT -ing!)
🗣️ Pronunciation
/aɪm ˈɡənə ˈvɪzɪt/
"going to" → gonna /ˈɡənə/
in fast, natural speech!
🔁 Drill: visit → gonna visit → I'm gonna visit

Did you discover these? Fixed future arrangements

Clarify ✏️
😊 Meaning
A fixed arrangement with a specific time/place, often with other people.
CCQs:
• Now or future? [Future]
• Is it arranged? [Yes]
• Specific time? [Yes]
🔧 Form
subject + am/is/are
+ verb-ing (+ time)
+ I'm meeting friends
− I'm not staying late
? What are you doing…?
⚠ Don't forget BE (am/is/are)!
🗣️ Pronunciation
/aɪm ˈmiːtɪŋ/
/wɒt ə jə ˈduːɪŋ/
Contraction: I'm /aɪm/
"are you" → /ə jə/
🔁 Drill: the weak forms + -ing /ɪŋ/
