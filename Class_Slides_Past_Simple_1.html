Meaning + Form

WHEN do we use Past Simple? HOW do we make it?

10 min ⏱️
💡 Meaning — Finished actions in the past
▶
5 years ago
yesterday
NOW
Time markers: yesterday · last week / month / year · two days ago · in 2020 · when I was a child
✓ Affirmative
Subject + verbpast
I walked to school.
She visited Paris.
We went home.
Regular: verb + ed
Irregular: change form (go→went)
✗ Negative
Subject + didn't + verbbase
I didn't walk to school.
She didn't visit Paris.
We didn't go home.
⚠️ Base verb (not past!)
❌ I didn't went · ✓ I didn't go
? Question
Did + subject + verbbase?
Did you walk?
Did she visit Paris?
Did we go home?
⚠️ Base verb after Did!
❌ Did you went · ✓ Did you go

Pronunciation — the 3 sounds of -ed

Regular verbs end in -ed, but they have THREE different sounds!

5 min ⏱️
/t/
SOUND 1
After voiceless sounds: /k/ /p/ /s/ /ʃ/ /tʃ/ /f/
walked · /wɔːkt/
watched · /wɒtʃt/
stopped · /stɒpt/
worked · /wɜːkt/
🗣️ Like a quick "t" — no extra syllable!
/d/
SOUND 2
After voiced sounds (and vowels): /l/ /n/ /v/ /b/ /g/
played · /pleɪd/
listened · /ˈlɪsənd/
lived · /lɪvd/
called · /kɔːld/
🗣️ Soft "d" sound — no extra syllable!
/ɪd/
SOUND 3
After /t/ and /d/ sounds only
wanted · /ˈwɒntɪd/
started · /ˈstɑːtɪd/
decided · /dɪˈsaɪdɪd/
needed · /ˈniːdɪd/
⚠️ Adds an EXTRA syllable: want-ID
