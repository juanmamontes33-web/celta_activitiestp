M

Anna: Of course! And hey, did you make a mistake in the science test yesterday?
M
Marco: Yes, one answer was wrong! But I'm also doing a project with Lisa this week, so I'm studying a lot.
A
Anna: That's great. I hope you do well in your next exam!
M
Marco: Me too! And what about you? Is it easy to make friends at your new job?
A
Anna: It's hard at first, but I'm trying!

SECTION 3

3 collocations to remember ⭐

Look at the meaning, the form, and the pronunciation!

📞
make a
phone call
Meaning
Use a phone to talk to someone.
Form
make + a + phone call (countable) · Past: made a phone call
Pron
/meɪk ə fəʊn kɔːl/ Stress on PHONE. "a" = weak /ə/
😅
make a
mistake
Meaning
Do something wrong by accident.
Form
make + a + mistake (countable) · Past: made a mistake
Pron
/meɪk ə mɪˈsteɪk/ Stress on -TAKE (not mis-!)
📚
do your
homework
Meaning
Complete the school work the teacher gives you to do at home.
Form
do + your/my + homework · ⚠ NO "a"! Uncountable! · Past: did your homework
Pron
/duː jə ˈhəʊmwɜːk/ Stress on HOME · "your" = weak /jə/
