⚖️

The big difference — TIME

It all comes down to one thing: do we say WHEN, or not?

Discover 🧭
Present Perfect
I have been to London.
✔️ an experience · we DON'T say when · connected to now
Past Simple
I went to London in 2022.
✔️ a finished action · we DO say when · a specific past time
🔑 No time said → Present Perfect  ·  Specific past time said → Past Simple
🚦

Signal words — your secret helpers

These words tell you which tense to use. Learn them!

Signals 🚦
🟩 Present Perfect words
ever never just already yet recently so far this week
→ time is unfinished or not said
🟧 Past Simple words
yesterday last week … ago in 2020 when I was… last night at 6 o'clock
→ a finished, specific past time
🎯 Find the signal word first — it almost always tells you the answer!
⛔

The #1 mistake — fix it forever!

This is the most common error. Learn it now and you'll never forget.

Rule ⛔
⛔ The golden rule
A finished time word (yesterday, ago, in 2020, last…)
= Past Simple. NEVER present perfect!
I have seen him yesterday. ❌
→ I saw him yesterday. ✅
I have visited Rome in 2019. ❌
→ I visited Rome in 2019. ✅
🇪🇸 In Spanish you might say "he visitado Roma en 2019" — but in English, the year forces the past simple!
🔁

The natural pattern — ask, then tell

This is how people really speak. Master this and you sound natural!

Pattern 🔁
Step 1 · Ask about experience
🟩 "Have you ever eaten sushi?"
➡️
Step 2 · Give the details
🟧 "Yes! I tried it in Tokyo last year."
⬅️
🔊 QUICK DRILL — repeat the connected sounds:
"Have you ever…?" → /həv juː ˈevə/  ·  "When did you…?" → /wen dɪdʒə/
⭐

Bonus words — just, already, yet

Three little words that LOVE the present perfect.

Bonus ⭐
just
= a very short time ago
"I've just arrived."
have + just + V3
already
= sooner than expected (+)
"I've already eaten."
have + already + V3
yet
= until now (? and −)
"Have you finished yet?"
…V3 + yet? / not …yet
⭐ These all connect the past to now — so they go with the present perfect, never a finished-time past!
