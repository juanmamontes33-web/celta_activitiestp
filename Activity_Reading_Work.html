Do You Still Need a Degree?

Careers & Education

1For decades, the path to a good job seemed obvious: study hard at school, go to university, get a degree, and the career would follow. But is that still true today?

2University is expensive. In many countries, students leave with a qualification but also with years of debt from tuition fees. Meanwhile, some of the world's most successful entrepreneurs — from tech founders to designers — never finished their degrees at all. Stories like these have made many young people wonder whether three or four years at university are really worth it.

3At the same time, the job market is changing fast. Employers increasingly say they care less about where you studied and more about what you can actually do. "Soft skills" like communication, teamwork and problem-solving are now in high demand, and these are not always taught in a lecture hall. A growing number of companies have dropped their degree requirements completely, focusing instead on practical experience.

4This has opened the door to alternatives. Apprenticeships let people earn a salary while they train on the job. Online courses allow anyone with an internet connection to pick up a new skill in weeks rather than years. Many programmers, marketers and photographers today are entirely self-taught.

5Of course, a degree still matters in many fields. You would not want a self-taught surgeon operating on you! For doctors, lawyers and engineers, formal qualifications are essential and always will be.

6Perhaps the real lesson is that learning no longer stops when we leave school. Whether at university, in an apprenticeship, or online at midnight, the people who succeed are the ones who never stop learning.
