Activity vs Result — the big idea

Same verb, different focus!

Discover 🧭
CONTINUOUS
"I've been painting the wall."
🖌️🎨
→ focus on the activity
→ maybe NOT finished (paint everywhere!)
→ answers "how long?"
SIMPLE
"I've painted the wall."
✅🧱
→ focus on the result
→ FINISHED (the wall is done!)
→ answers "how many / what's done?"
❓

How long? vs How many?

The question word is your biggest clue!

Clue 🔑
How long…? ⏳
→ duration → CONTINUOUS
"How long have you been reading?"
"For two hours."
How many…? 🔢
→ quantity → SIMPLE
"How many books have you read?"
"Three books."
⏳ how long + a period → continuous  ·  🔢 how many + a number → simple
🔎

Two everyday uses

The "why are you…?" trick and the "look!" trick.

Use 🔎
🔵 Explain how you look/feel
"You look tired! Why?"
→ "I've been working all day."
The activity explains the present result. ⏳
🟢 Show a finished result
"Look! 🎉
I've finished the report!"
It's done — here is the result. ✅
⛔

Careful! Some verbs say NO to -ing

Stative verbs use the SIMPLE form only.

Watch out ⛔
Verbs about states (not actions) don't use the continuous:
know like / love be have (= own) understand believe
I've been knowing her for years. ❌
I've known her for years. ✅
