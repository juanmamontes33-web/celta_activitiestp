Active → Passive 🔄
Active — focus on the doer
A local family runs the café.
Passive — focus on the action
The café is run (by a local family).
Form: subject + be + past participle ( + by + agent ). The tense lives in be. Drop by… when the doer is unknown, obvious or unimportant.
Present simpleThe café is run by a family.
Past simpleThe old town was built centuries ago.
Present perfectSlow travel has been chosen by millions.
Future (will)Breakfast will be served at eight.
ModalThe room can be cleaned quickly.
