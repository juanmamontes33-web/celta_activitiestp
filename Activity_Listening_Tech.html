📄 Transcript — teacher reference / post-listening

Neil: Hello. This is 6 Minute English from BBC Learning English. I'm Neil. Beth: What are you doing? Get off your phone! …And I'm Beth. Are you addicted to social media? With so many apps out there — Snapchat, TikTok, and the latest, Threads — it's easy to spend a lot more time on your phone than ever before.

There are plenty of studies looking at how social media affects mental health, with some saying it can be as addictive as gambling. Research in the US found that adolescents who spend more than three hours a day on social media have double the risk of developing depression and anxiety. An adolescent is someone aged 10 to 19, between childhood and adulthood. The US is in the process of regulating social media apps for teenagers; some scientists think the UK should do the same.

Surveys reveal social media makes teens feel worse about their body image, and 64% of teens say they are regularly exposed to hate-based content. Quiz: What percentage of 13–24 year-olds use Snapchat — 70%, 80% or 90%? (Answer at the end: 90%.)

Professor Devi Sridhar (Chair of global public health, University of Edinburgh) on BBC World Service's Inside Science: young girls are being "fed" content about eating disorders in an addictive way; the algorithm keeps giving them that content because it keeps them on their phones — and the apps' revenue comes from advertising. To be "fed" content = to be given it automatically. Algorithms = a complex set of rules that prioritise and personalise the content a user sees. Revenue = the money a company earns.

She adds that the "onus" (the responsibility) has been put on parents to find solutions — debates with your child are "a losing battle" (a fight you cannot win) because social media is their entire social network. Answer: 90% of 13–24 year-olds use Snapchat.
