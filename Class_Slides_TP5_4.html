Are you a celeb worshipper?

Adapted from the Daily Mail · by James Chapman

Read 📖
Do you wake up thinking about David Beckham, or wonder what Brad Pitt has for breakfast? If so, you might suffer from "Celebrity Worship Syndrome" (CWS) — a recently identified psychological condition. According to psychologists at the University of Leicester, around a third of people are so obsessed with someone famous that the obsession affects their daily life.

One theory is that in a society dominated by TV, where extended families and communities are weaker, celebrities have taken the place of relatives, neighbours and friends.

The study of around 700 people aged 18 to 60 identified three types of celebrity worshipper. People with the mild form (22%) were sociable extroverts whose worst symptom was talking endlessly about their favourite star. A moderate group (12%) felt an intense personal identification with their idol. The most serious group — around 2% — were solitary and impulsive, believed their celebrity truly knew them, and were prepared to lie, or even die, for their hero.

2. "According to the article, …"
3. "The article claims that…"
4. "It raises the question of whether…"
