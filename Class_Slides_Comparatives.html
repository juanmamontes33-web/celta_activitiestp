Comparatives02 / 11
MEANING
When we use it · comparing two
We compare two things and show the difference. 🆚
"A car is faster than a bike."
❓ Faster — car or bike? Car
❓ Two things or three? Two
"This phone is more expensive than that one."
❓ Which costs more? This one
❓ Do we always add than? Yes — to say than what
🇪🇸 Spanish uses más … que for everything — but English has two patterns. Let's see them! 👇
Comparatives03 / 11
FORM · 1
Short adjectives · add -er
1-syllable adjectives → add -er (+ than):
cheap → cheaper  ·  fast → faster  ·  old → older  ·  tall → taller
⚠ Watch the spelling:
ends in -e
nice → nicer
large → larger
vowel + consonant
big → bigger
hot → hotter
consonant + -y
happy → happier
easy → easier
Comparatives04 / 11
FORM · 2
Long adjectives & irregulars
Long adjectives (2–3 syllables) → use more (+ than):
expensive → more expensive  ·  modern → more modern  ·  interesting → more interesting
And a few irregulars to learn by heart:
good → better
bad → worse
far → further
Comparatives05 / 11
STRETCH · B2
Make it bigger or smaller · modifiers
How big is the difference? 📏
BIG DIFFERENCE
much / far / a lot
"It's much cheaper." "far bigger."
SMALL DIFFERENCE
a bit / a little / slightly
"It's a bit more expensive."
💡 Pattern: much / a bit + comparative.  "This laptop is much lighter and a bit more expensive."

SOUND
Say it naturally · the music of comparatives
-ER = SCHWA /ə/
bigger /ˈbɪɡə/
NOT "big-ERR" — soft, weak ending
THAN = WEAK /ðən/
than /ðən/
soft "th" 👅 — not "dan" or "tan"
STRESS
on the adjective
not on "than" or "-er"
↻ DRILL — repeat, keeping "than" weak
"It's CHEAPer than THIS one."  ·  "She's TALLer than ME."

Comparatives07 / 11
READ ALOUD
Read in pairs · comparatives + pronunciation
💬 Buying a phone
A: This phone is cheaper than that one, but that one has a better camera.
B: True, but the cheaper one is much lighter, and the battery lasts longer.
A: The expensive one is faster than both, though.
B: Faster, yes — but it's a better deal. It's smaller and easier to carry.
A: You're right. Let's get that one!
💬 City or countryside?
A: Do you prefer the city or the countryside?
B: The countryside! It's quieter than the city and the air is cleaner.
A: But the city is more exciting. It's easier to find a job.
B: True, but city life is much more expensive than country life.
A: I suppose a slower life is a happier life!
🎯 Swap roles and read again. Keep -er soft /ə/ and than weak /ðən/. Stress the adjective!
