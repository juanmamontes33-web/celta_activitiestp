Step 1 · Notice

Read the conversation 📖

Anna and Marco are talking about the weekend. First read quickly and answer the question — then go hunting for the future phrases!

A
Anna: Hi Marco! What are you doing this weekend? Do you have any plans?
M
Marco: Hi Anna! Yes, I'm quite busy. On Saturday I'm meeting some friends in the centre at eleven o'clock. We're having lunch at a new Italian restaurant.
A
Anna: That sounds lovely! Are you going to do anything special after lunch?
M
Marco: Yes — I'm going to visit the new art exhibition. I really want to see it. What about you? What are you going to do?
A
Anna: I'm not sure yet. I think I'm going to relax at home. But on Sunday I'm playing tennis with my sister at nine o'clock — we booked the court last week.
M
Marco: Nice! Are you going to watch the football match on Sunday evening too?
A
Anna: Maybe — I haven't decided. Oh, I almost forgot: I'm seeing the dentist on Monday at eight o'clock, so I'm not staying out late.
M
Marco: Good plan. Have a great weekend — I'm going to call you on Sunday to tell you about the exhibition!

Step 2 · Discover the MEANING

When do we use each one? 🔎

Some plans are fixed arrangements (a specific time/place, often with other people). Others are just plans or intentions you have decided. Read each sentence and decide — you work it out!
