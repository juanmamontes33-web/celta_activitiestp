Two witnesses, two stories

Read the conversation. Don't worry about every word — just get the main idea, then answer the two questions.
Mara:Did you hear about the crash outside the café yesterday?
Dan:A bit — but I've heard two completely different versions, actually.
Mara:Same here! So, according to the waiter, a cyclist was just riding past when a car door suddenly opened right in front of him.
Dan:Right. And all of a sudden the cyclist flew over the handlebars and landed on the pavement.
Mara:Exactly. The waiter claimed that the driver wasn't even looking.
Dan:But the old lady at the bus stop told a completely different story. She was absolutely certain that the cyclist was going way too fast.
Mara:Hmm. So whose fault was it?
Dan:Honestly? Nobody knows. In the end, the cyclist stood up, picked up his bike and just walked away.
Mara:Lucky him! Two witnesses — and two completely different stories.
