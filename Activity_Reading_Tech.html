Are We Addicted to Our Screens?

Digital Culture

1Think about the first thing you did this morning. For millions of people, the answer is simple: they reached for their phone before they even got out of bed. Checking notifications has become an automatic habit — and that is no accident.

2Social media apps are carefully designed to keep us coming back. Every time we open them, we never know exactly what we'll find: a funny video, a message from a friend, or a photo that makes us laugh. This unpredictable reward is the same mechanism that makes gambling so addictive. The more we scroll, the more our brains expect that little hit of pleasure.

3Tech companies are well aware of this. Features like "infinite scroll" and the red notification dot are not there by chance; they are designed to capture our attention and hold on to it for as long as possible. After all, in what experts call "the attention economy", our time is the product being sold to advertisers.

4The effects are not all negative. Social media helps us stay in touch with people far away, discover new ideas, and build communities around shared interests. For many, it is a source of support and creativity. However, a growing number of users are starting to push back. "Digital detox" weekends, screen-time limits and apps that block other apps are becoming increasingly popular, especially among young people who grew up online.

5Perhaps the real question is not whether technology is good or bad, but whether we are the ones in control. The next time your phone lights up, ask yourself: am I choosing to look — or is the app choosing for me?
